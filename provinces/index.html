China’s 34 Provincial‑Level Divisions

23 Provinces · 4 Municipalities · 5 Autonomous Regions · 2 SARs — every name tells a story

❄️ Northeast (东北)

黑龙江
Heilongjiang
Hēilóngjiāng
Black Dragon River
Named after the Heilong River (Amur), which marks the border with Russia.
吉林
Jilin
Jílín
Auspicious Forest (from Manchu "girin ula")
Shortened from "Jilin Ula", a Manchu phrase meaning "along the river."
辽宁
Liaoning
Liáoníng
Liao River + Peace
The Liao River runs through the province; "ning" expresses a wish for peace.

🏯 North (华北)

北京
Beijing (M)
Běijīng
Northern Capital
The capital in the north; contrast with Nanjing ("Southern Capital").
天津
Tianjin (M)
Tiānjīn
Heaven's Ford
Named by a Ming emperor after an imperial ferry crossing.
河北
Hebei
Héběi
North of the River
The river is the Yellow River (黄河). North of it = Hebei.
山西
Shanxi
Shānxī
West of the Mountains
The mountains are the Taihang Range. West side = Shanxi.
内蒙古
Inner Mongolia (AR)
Nèi Měnggǔ
Inner Mongolia
"Inner" distinguishes it from Outer Mongolia, the independent country.

🌊 East (华东)

上海
Shanghai (M)
Shànghǎi
Upon the Sea
Originally a fishing village on the East China Sea coast.
江苏
Jiangsu
Jiāngsū
Jiang (River) + Su (from Suzhou)
Combines "Yangtze River" and the old city of Suzhou.
浙江
Zhejiang
Zhèjiāng
Crooked River
Named after the Qiantang River's winding shape.
安徽
Anhui
Ānhuī
Peace + (Hui from Huizhou)
Combines Anqing city and Huizhou prefecture.
福建
Fujian
Fújiàn
Fortune + Established
Name comes from Fuzhou and Jian'ou, two ancient cities.
江西
Jiangxi
Jiāngxī
West of the River
The river is the Yangtze; historically called "Jiangnan West."
山东
Shandong
Shāndōng
East of the Mountains
The mountains are the Taihang Range; east side = Shandong.
台湾
Taiwan
Táiwān
Terrace Bay
Likely from the aboriginal Siraya word "Tayovan."

🏞️ Central (华中)

河南
Henan
Hénán
South of the River
The river is the Yellow River. Most of Henan lies south of it.
湖北
Hubei
Húběi
North of the Lake
The lake is Dongting Lake. Hubei = north of it.
湖南
Hunan
Húnán
South of the Lake
Again, Dongting Lake. Hunan = south of it.

🌴 South (华南)

广东
Guangdong
Guǎngdōng
Wide East
"Guang" refers to the region; east of Guangxi.
广西
Guangxi (AR)
Guǎngxī
Wide West
Now the Guangxi Zhuang Autonomous Region.
海南
Hainan
Hǎinán
South of the Sea
Island province; "sea" refers to the South China Sea.
香港
Hong Kong (SAR)
Xiānggǎng
Fragrant Harbor
Named after its historic incense trade and deep port.
澳门
Macau (SAR)
Àomén
Bay Gate
Refers to the A-Ma Temple (sea goddess) at the harbor entrance.

⛰️ Southwest (西南)

重庆
Chongqing (M)
Chóngqìng
Double Celebration
A Southern Song emperor celebrated being made prince then emperor here.
四川
Sichuan
Sìchuān
Four Rivers
Refers to the four major river basins in the region.
贵州
Guizhou
Guìzhōu
Noble Prefecture
The name comes from the ancient "Gui" mountain.
云南
Yunnan
Yúnnán
South of the Clouds
"Cloud" refers to the Yunling Mountains; "south" = south of them.
西藏
Tibet (AR)
Xīzàng
Western Treasure / Tibet
"Zang" refers to the Tibetan people; "xi" means west.

🏜️ Northwest (西北)

陕西
Shaanxi
Shǎnxī
West of the Shan (Plateau)
"Shan" refers to the Shanzhou plateau; west of it.
甘肃
Gansu
Gānsù
Sweet + Solemn
Combines Ganzhou and Suzhou, two ancient cities.
青海
Qinghai
Qīnghǎi
Blue‑Green Sea
Named after the vast Qinghai Lake (the largest in China).
宁夏
Ningxia (AR)
Níngxià
Peaceful Xia
Wishes for peace in the former Western Xia kingdom.
新疆
Xinjiang (AR)
Xīnjiāng
New Frontier
Officially incorporated under Qing rule; the "new border."
