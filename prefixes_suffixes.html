📖 What Are Chinese Affixes?

In Mandarin Chinese, 词缀 (cízhuì) — affixes — are morphemes that attach to root words to modify meaning, create new words, or indicate grammatical functions. Unlike English, Chinese affixes are often semi-productive and many originated from full-content words that underwent grammaticalization over centuries.

💡 Key Insight: Many Chinese "affixes" can still function as standalone words. For example, 子 (zǐ) means "child" on its own, but as a suffix in 桌子 (zhuōzi · table) it's a neutral nominal marker. This dual nature is a hallmark of Chinese morphology.

Prefixes (前缀) appear before the root — e.g., 老- in 老师 (lǎoshī · teacher). Suffixes (后缀) appear after the root — e.g., -子 in 椅子 (yǐzi · chair). Some affixes are highly productive (like -性 for "-ness"), while others are limited to specific lexicalized words. Below you'll find a curated collection of the most important ones, organized for easy reference.

➕ Common Chinese Prefixes (前缀 qiánzhuì)

Prefixes attach before a root to indicate familiarity, ordinality, negation, degree, or scope.

老 lǎo-

Familiarity or respect; also used for animals and ordinal seniority.

老师 lǎoshī · teacher 老虎 lǎohǔ · tiger 老王 Lǎo Wáng · Old Wang 老大 lǎodà · eldest / boss
小 xiǎo-

Diminutive; informal/friendly address; also "little" or "young."

小王 Xiǎo Wáng · Little Wang 小猫 xiǎomāo · kitten 小姐 xiǎojiě · Miss 小吃 xiǎochī · snack
第 dì-

Forms ordinal numbers (first, second, third…).

第一 dìyī · first 第二 dì'èr · second 第三十 dìsānshí · thirtieth
初 chū-

Ordinal for days 1–10 of the lunar month; "beginning / early."

初一 chūyī · 1st day 初雪 chūxuě · first snow 初步 chūbù · preliminary
阿 ā-

Affectionate/familiar prefix (common in southern dialects).

阿妈 āmā · mom 阿明 Ā Míng · (name) 阿姨 āyí · auntie
可 kě-

"-able / -ible"; worth doing; also conveys emphasis.

可爱 kě'ài · lovable / cute 可怕 kěpà · fearful / scary 可笑 kěxiào · laughable 可靠 kěkào · reliable
非 fēi-

"Non- / un- / in-"; negation or exclusion.

非法 fēifǎ · illegal 非常 fēicháng · extraordinary / very 非正式 fēizhèngshì · informal
超 chāo-

"Super- / ultra- / hyper-"; exceeding the norm.

超级 chāojí · super 超人 chāorén · superman 超速 chāosù · speeding
自 zì-

"Self- / auto-"; reflexive or automatic action.

自己 zìjǐ · oneself 自动 zìdòng · automatic 自学 zìxué · self-study
反 fǎn-

"Anti- / counter-"; opposition or reversal.

反对 fǎnduì · oppose 反常 fǎncháng · abnormal 反作用 fǎnzuòyòng · reaction
多 duō-

"Multi- / poly- / many"; plurality or abundance.

多元 duōyuán · multivariate 多媒体 duōméitǐ · multimedia 多功能 duōgōngnéng · multifunctional
前 qián-

"Pre- / former / ex-"; prior in time or position.

前总统 qiánzǒngtǒng · former president 前天 qiántiān · day before yesterday 前言 qiányán · preface
微 wēi-

"Micro- / tiny"; small scale or subtlety.

微信 Wēixìn · WeChat 微观 wēiguān · microscopic 微笑 wēixiào · smile

➖ Common Chinese Suffixes (后缀 hòuzhuì)

Suffixes attach after a root to form nouns, indicate plural, express abstract qualities, or mark grammatical roles.

子 -zi

Neutral nominal suffix — the most common suffix; turns roots into concrete nouns. Tone becomes neutral.

桌子 zhuōzi · table 孩子 háizi · child 房子 fángzi · house 鼻子 bízi · nose
儿 -r / -ér

Diminutive / rhotacization — especially common in northern dialects (Beijing); adds lightness or smallness.

花儿 huār · flower 小孩儿 xiǎoháir · little kid 一点儿 yìdiǎnr · a little bit 玩儿 wánr · to play
头 -tou

Nominal suffix for concrete objects or abstract concepts; tone becomes neutral.

石头 shítou · stone 木头 mùtou · wood / log 念头 niàntou · thought / idea 甜头 tiántou · benefit / sweetness
们 -men

Plural marker for people (and sometimes personified animals/things).

我们 wǒmen · we / us 朋友们 péngyoumen · friends 孩子们 háizimen · children
性 -xìng

"-ness / -ity / nature of" — highly productive for abstract nouns.

重要性 zhòngyàoxìng · importance 可能性 kěnéngxìng · possibility 安全性 ānquánxìng · safety
化 -huà

"-ize / -ify / -ization" — transformation or change into a state.

现代化 xiàndàihuà · modernization 自动化 zìdònghuà · automation 全球化 quánqiúhuà · globalization
家 -jiā

"-ist / -er / specialist" — expert in a field.

科学家 kēxuéjiā · scientist 作家 zuòjiā · writer / author 艺术家 yìshùjiā · artist
者 -zhě

"-er / -or / one who" — agent or person associated with an action.

读者 dúzhě · reader 作者 zuòzhě · author 记者 jìzhě · journalist
员 -yuán

Member / personnel — person belonging to an organization or role.

演员 yǎnyuán · actor 服务员 fúwùyuán · waiter / server 运动员 yùndòngyuán · athlete
度 -dù

Degree / measure — extent or level of something.

温度 wēndù · temperature 速度 sùdù · speed 难度 nándù · difficulty
学 -xué

"-ology / study of" — field of academic study.

科学 kēxué · science 数学 shùxué · mathematics 语言学 yǔyánxué · linguistics
感 -gǎn

Feeling / sense of — perceptual or emotional quality.

安全感 ānquángǎn · sense of security 美感 měigǎn · aesthetic sense 责任感 zérèngǎn · sense of responsibility

💡 Important Notes & Insights

🔍 Free vs. Bound Morphemes: In Chinese, many affixes are not fully bound — they can appear as independent words. For instance, 学 (xué) means "to learn" as a verb, but acts as a suffix meaning "-ology" in 数学 (shùxué · mathematics). This flexibility is a key feature of isolating languages like Chinese.
🗣️ Regional Variation — The -儿 Suffix: The suffix -儿 (-r) is especially prominent in Beijing and northern Chinese dialects (the rhotacized "érhuà" 儿化). In southern China, this suffix is rarely used. For example, 花 (huā) in the south vs. 花儿 (huār) in the north — both mean "flower," but the northern form adds the retroflex ending.
📈 Productivity: Some suffixes like -性 (-xìng) and -化 (-huà) are highly productive — new words are constantly coined using them (e.g., 数字化 shùzìhuà · digitization). Others like -子 (-zi) and -头 (-tou) are mostly lexicalized, meaning they appear in fixed words and aren't used to create new ones freely.
🎯 Tone Neutralization: When 子, 头, and 们 function as suffixes, they lose their original tone and become neutral (轻声 qīngshēng). For example, 子 is zǐ (3rd tone) as a standalone word ("child"), but becomes neutral zi in 桌子 (zhuōzi).
