汉语语法 — Beginner's Path

The most important structures to start speaking Chinese today.

🏗️ Basic Sentence Structure (SVO)

Chinese word order is Subject–Verb–Object — same as English. No conjugation!

The core pattern is Subject + Verb + Object. Verbs never change form, so you don't need to worry about tenses through the verb itself.

SVO
我 爱 你。
Wǒ ài nǐ.
I love you.
SVO
他 吃 苹果。
Tā chī píngguǒ.
He eats an apple.

Topic-Comment (The "As for…" Pattern)

Chinese often starts with the topic (what you're talking about), followed by a comment. This feels natural even if it breaks SVO.

Topic-Comment
中国菜，我 很 喜欢。
Zhōngguó cài, wǒ hěn xǐhuān.
Chinese food — I really like it.

🔤 Essential Verbs: 是, 有, 在

These three tiny words do the heavy lifting for "to be", "to have" and "to be at".

是 (shì) – to be

Used to link two nouns (identity, category).

我 是 学生。
Wǒ shì xuésheng.
I am a student.

有 (yǒu) – to have

Possession or existence.

我 有 一个 哥哥。
Wǒ yǒu yī gè gēge.
I have one older brother.

在 (zài) – to be at / in

Location or ongoing action (we'll keep it simple: location).

他 在 家。
Tā zài jiā.
He is at home.
💡 Remember: 是 is not used with adjectives. You don't say "我是高" — instead you say "我 很 高" (I am tall). We'll cover adjectives later.

❓ Asking Questions

No complicated word order changes — just add a small particle or use question words in place.

① 吗 (ma) – Yes/No questions

Take any statement and put 吗 at the end.

你 好 → 你 好 吗？
Nǐ hǎo → Nǐ hǎo ma?
Hello → How are you? (lit. "Are you good?")

② 呢 (ne) – "What about…?"

Bounce the question back with 呢.

我 很好，你 呢？
Wǒ hěn hǎo, nǐ ne?
I'm good. And you?

③ 吧 (ba) – Suggestions

Softens a statement into a suggestion.

我们 走 吧。
Wǒmen zǒu ba.
Let's go.

④ Question Words (keep the same order)

📏 Measure Words (量词 liàngcí)

You can't say "a book" — you say "one [measure word] book".

Pattern: Number + Measure Word + Noun

MWUsed forExample
个 (gè)General / people一个人 (yī gè rén) — one person
本 (běn)Books一本书 (yī běn shū) — one book
杯 (bēi)Cups / drinks一杯茶 (yī bēi chá) — a cup of tea
张 (zhāng)Flat objects一张纸 (yī zhāng zhǐ) — a sheet of paper
只 (zhī)Animals, one of a pair一只猫 (yī zhī māo) — a cat
条 (tiáo)Long, flexible一条鱼 (yī tiáo yú) — a fish
辆 (liàng)Vehicles一辆车 (yī liàng chē) — a car
👍 When unsure, use 个 (gè) — it's the universal fallback and works in most everyday situations.

🧩 Key Particles: 的 & 了

Tiny words that mark possession, description, or completed actions.

的 (de) — Possession & Description

的 connects a possessor to a thing, or an adjective to a noun.

我 的 手机
Wǒ de shǒujī
My mobile phone
很 好吃 的 菜
Hěn hǎochī de cài
A delicious dish (lit. "very tasty 的 dish")

了 (le) — Completed Action or Change

了 often appears after a verb (completed) or at the end of a sentence (new situation).

Completed
我 买 了 三 本 书。
Wǒ mǎi le sān běn shū.
I bought three books.
Change
我 累 了。
Wǒ lèi le.
I'm tired (now). / I've become tired.

🚫 Negation: 不 (bù) vs 没 (méi)

Two different "no" words — choose based on time and meaning.

不 (bù)

Present & Future
Habits, adjectives, future actions.

我 不 喝 咖啡。
Wǒ bù hē kāfēi.
I don't drink coffee.
他 不 忙。
Tā bù máng.
He is not busy.

没 (méi)

Past actions / 有
Negates completed actions and 有.

我 没 去。
Wǒ méi qù.
I didn't go.
我 没 有 车。
Wǒ méi yǒu chē.
I don't have a car.

⏰ Time Words & Word Order

Time expressions usually come before the verb — at the start or right after the subject.

Pattern: Subject + Time + Verb (+ Object)

我 明天 去 上海。
Wǒ míngtiān qù Shànghǎi.
I'm going to Shanghai tomorrow.
昨天 他 没 来。
Zuótiān tā méi lái.
Yesterday he didn't come.

Common time words: 今天 (jīntiān - today), 明天 (míngtiān - tomorrow), 昨天 (zuótiān - yesterday), 现在 (xiànzài - now), 每天 (měitiān - every day)

🌈 Adjectives – No "to be" Needed

Adjectives behave like verbs — you don't use 是. Use 很 (hěn) to link them naturally.

她 很 漂亮。
Tā hěn piàoliang.
She is beautiful. (lit. "She very beautiful")
这个 苹果 很 大。
Zhège píngguǒ hěn dà.
This apple is big.
⚠️ Never say "她是漂亮". Always insert 很 (even if you don't mean "very") — it makes the sentence sound complete.

Plurals: 们 (men)

Add 们 to pronouns or people nouns (never objects).

我 (wǒ) — I→ 我们 (wǒmen) — We
你 (nǐ) — You→ 你们 (nǐmen) — You (plural)
他/她 (tā)→ 他们/她们 (tāmen) — They
朋友 (péngyou)→ 朋友们 (péngyoumen) — Friends
